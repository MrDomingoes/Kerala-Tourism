KANNUR

ABOUT DISTRICT 

Kannur: The Largest district in kerala

Kannur District is one of the 14 districts along the west coast in the state of Kerala, India. The town of Kannur is the district headquarters and gives the district its name. The old name, Cannanore is the anglicised form of the Malayalam name "Kannur". Kannur district is bounded by Kasaragod District to the north, Kozhikode district to the south and Wayanad District to the southeast. To the east the district is bounded by the Western Ghats, which forms the border with the state of Karnataka (Kodagu district). The Arabian Sea is to the west. The district was established in 1957.

Places Of interest

Paithalmala

Paithalmala (Vaithalmala) is a hill station located in the Kannur district of Kerala. This section joins the Western Ghats, passing through the trekking forest and extending to the summit.

Location: Kudiyanmala, Kerala

Muzhappilangad Beach

Muzhappilangad beach is the only driving beach in Kerala. This place is located at a distance of 7 km from Thalassery in Kannur.

Location: Muzhappilangad Beach Rd, Muzhappilangad

Payyambalam Beach

Payyambalam Beach in Kannur is a well maintained place. This place is the perfect place to enjoy a family picnic or relax. For those who want a little adventure.

Location: Kannur

Kannur Fort

The fort was built by Don Francesco de Almeida (1505), the first Portuguese Viceroy of India. St. Angelo's Fort in Kannur is of historical significance.

Location: Near Cannannore Cantonment, Burnacherry

St Angelo Fort

St Angelo's Fort is definitely one of the most popular and frequently visited attractions of Kannur and for more reasons than one. The polished yet prehistoric structure of this fortress oozes with unadulterated elegance and overwhelming power.

Location: Near Cannannore Cantonment

Kannur Lighthouse

Being Kerala's first lighthouse ever and Kannur's only one, the Kannur Lighthouse is a pretty big deal. This cylindrical concrete tower has a balcony with a light that is lit as the evening breaks in to alert the seamen of the land.

Location: Burnacherry, Kannur

Parassinikkadavu Snake Park

Regarded as one of the finest snake sanctuaries of Asia, the Parassinikadavu Snake Park is the pride of the south. This snake park is one of the most prominent reptile preservation centres in the country and is the only one in Kerala. It is home to over 150 different species of reptiles like crocodiles, monitor lizards and snakes- both venomous and non-venomous.

Location: Parassinikkadavu - Mayyil Rd

Palakkayam Thattu

ucked away in the lush greenery of the Western Ghats and soaring at a dizzying altitude of 3500 feet above sea level, Palakayyam Thattu is one of the most beautiful hill stations in the entire state of Kerala. find.

Location: Naduvil kottayamthattu road Palakkayam
